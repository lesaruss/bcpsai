Nav restructure: recommendation and rationale

Sean asked for a best-practice recommendation here rather than a specific direction. This is what was implemented.

Primary nav now mirrors BCPS's own six-folder order

Recommendation: Home, Our Principles, Approved Tools, Parent Resources, Learning, AI Liaison Network, Community, Accountability. "Policy & Privacy" is relabeled "Parent Resources" to match BCPS's SharePoint folder name; its existing privacy FAQ content is unchanged and it is where the Digital Learning Blueprint will land once BCPS settles the disclosure question. "Sources" was removed from the primary tab bar and moved to a footer link, alongside the new "For Staff & Leaders" link.

Why: BCPS's own content is now the source of truth for this site's shape, per the content map's recommendation to adopt their six-folder structure as the backbone rather than keep our invented scaffolding. Two kinds of pages do not fit that backbone and were handled differently on purpose: "Sources" is a methodology and bibliography page (transparency about how the site was built), which is useful but is not something a parent needs in the main tab bar. "For Staff & Leaders" is a different audience entirely from the site's stated parent and community purpose. Standard information-architecture practice is to keep a primary nav focused on the site's primary audience and primary content, and to make secondary, different-audience, or meta content reachable but not competing for space, typically through a footer link or a linked CTA. Accountability stayed in the primary nav despite not being one of BCPS's six folders, because it is one of BCPS's own five stated commitments (Transparency, Trust, Education, Engagement, Accountability) in the source framework, not an add-on from us.
